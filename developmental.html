Developmental Psychology

Explore the fascinating journey of human development across the lifespan, from infancy through late adulthood. Understand the physical, cognitive, and social changes that shape who we become.

Life Stages

Infancy (0-2 years)

A period of rapid physical and cognitive development:

  • Development of motor skills
  • Formation of basic attachments
  • Beginning of language acquisition
  • Sensorimotor exploration

Adolescence (12-18 years)

Major changes include:

  • Identity formation
  • Abstract thinking
  • Emotional development
  • Physical maturation

Developmental Theories

Piaget's Theory

Four stages of cognitive development:

  • Sensorimotor (0-2 years)
  • Preoperational (2-7 years)
  • Concrete Operational (7-11 years)
  • Formal Operational (11+ years)

Erikson's Theory

Eight stages of psychosocial development:

  • Trust vs. Mistrust
  • Autonomy vs. Shame
  • Initiative vs. Guilt
  • Industry vs. Inferiority

Vygotsky's Theory

Social development theory:

  • Zone of Proximal Development
  • Scaffolding
  • Social Learning
  • Cultural Context

Quick Fact

Did you know? The brain develops from back to front, with the prefrontal cortex being the last to fully develop.

Early Childhood (2-7 years)

Key developments include:

  • Symbolic thought
  • Language expansion
  • Social skill development
  • Imagination and creativity

Middle Childhood (7-11 years)

Characterized by:

  • Logical thinking
  • Academic skills
  • Peer relationships
  • Self-awareness
